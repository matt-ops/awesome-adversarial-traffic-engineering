# Role narrative worksheet

Use evidence that is true for you. The sample spine is a structure, not text to memorize.

## 30-second version

I bring a network foundation and experience converting ambiguous security telemetry into detections, incident decisions, and durable remediation. I am applying that strength to adversarial traffic research: controlled browser and traffic experiments, false-positive-aware detection analysis, and findings with measurable retests.

## 60-second version

Add one concrete investigation example, one cross-team defensive improvement, and the browser/edge skills currently being demonstrated in the AATE artifacts. End with why the role’s offense-to-defense loop fits your working style.

## 90-second version

Cover: network engineering → detection engineering → incident response and threat hunting → AI-enabled workflows → controlled adversarial traffic research. Name the browser-research gap directly, show current evidence, and avoid claiming specialty depth before it is demonstrated.

## Core prompts

- Why this role?
- Why now?
- Why you despite the browser-research gap?
- What is your differentiator?
- What are you actively learning, and what evidence shows progress?

## Informational introduction

Keep the written request short: current role, two relevant strengths, the specific role question, and a request for a brief conversation. Do not include internal details in public repository artifacts.

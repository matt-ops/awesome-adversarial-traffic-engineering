# Research ethics

Defensive research should minimize harm, collect only necessary evidence, protect legitimate populations, and make uncertainty visible.

Use synthetic identities and data. Avoid capturing secrets, content, or persistent identifiers when aggregate or derived evidence is sufficient. Treat privacy tools, accessibility technology, test automation, shared networks, unusual devices, and degraded clients as legitimate populations that a detector may harm.

Do not turn a local learning artifact into a third-party bypass recipe. Report mechanisms, limitations, remediation, and validation rather than branding a client “undetectable” or a detector universal.

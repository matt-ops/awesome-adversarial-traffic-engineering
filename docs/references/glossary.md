# Glossary

**Adversary objective**  
The outcome the operator is trying to cause, such as reserving inventory or
degrading an application health objective.

**Protected action**  
The server-side state change or service effect a control is intended to prevent.

**Blocked baseline**  
Evidence that the simplest representative adversary was denied, challenged, or
throttled by the control being studied.

**Relied-upon signal**  
A client, protocol, session, behavior, or workflow observation that appears to
influence the control decision. Observation does not prove causality.

**Coherent signal set**  
Related values that must change together to represent one plausible environment,
such as locale, timezone, viewport, screen, platform, and browser claim.

**Residual anomaly**  
An inconsistency or unexplained observation that remains after a bypass or score
change.

**Legitimate near-neighbor**  
A benign client or workflow sufficiently similar to the attack to expose
collateral blocking.

**Exact retest**  
The same objective, protected action, attacker procedure, and evidence schema
repeated after remediation.

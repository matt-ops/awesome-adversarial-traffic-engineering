# Module 8: Interview communication and role translation

## Why this matters

The role requires clear judgment under follow-up. Communication should translate prior evidence into domain language, explain tradeoffs, show personal ownership, and distinguish current competence from the next learning step.

## Role outcomes

You can present a credible role narrative, technical deep dive, threat model, system design, coding and review approach, behavioral evidence, and first-90-day plan grounded in artifacts from Modules 0–7.

## Level 1: Foundation, 24-hour checkpoint

### Knowledge outcome

Know the structure of a role narrative, technical explanation, threat-model answer, basic bot-detection architecture, and evidence-rich behavioral story. Translate networking, detection, incident response, hunting, and reporting experience into adversarial traffic outcomes.

### Hands-on outcome

Draft and rehearse a 90-second role narrative, three behavioral stories, and a five-minute basic bot-detection architecture explanation using Foundation artifacts.

### Interview outcome

Answer “Why this role, why now, and why you despite the browser gap?” with specific transferable evidence and an honest current learning boundary.

### Required artifact

Recorded or written role narrative, three story outlines, and five-minute architecture answer.

### Completion test

Deliver the narrative in 90 seconds, name personal actions and measured outcomes in each story, and explain architecture without reading or listing tools as a substitute for mechanisms.

### Estimated time

1 focused hour of structured preparation; rehearse again outside the 24-hour curriculum budget as needed.

## Level 2: Applied, 7-day checkpoint

### Knowledge outcome

Structure domain answers, threat models, system designs, coding explanations, secure-review findings, and behavioral stories with assumptions, alternatives, metrics, tradeoffs, safety, and follow-up depth.

### Hands-on outcome

Prepare six behavioral stories, complete one bot/browser domain mock and one DDoS/edge mock, and revise answers using scorecard evidence.

### Interview outcome

Sustain a 30-minute screen, recognize a question’s depth, clarify assumptions, and connect answers to Applied artifacts rather than hypothetical expertise.

### Required artifact

Six story worksheets, two recordings or transcripts, two scorecards, and a prioritized gap log.

### Completion test

Each mock meets the scorecard threshold for structure, measurement, safety, tradeoffs, and communication with no critical unsupported claim.

### Estimated time

5 additional focused hours.

## Level 3: Integrated, 21-day checkpoint

### Knowledge outcome

Integrate practical Python, secure review, bot/browser domain, DDoS/edge design, reporting, and behavioral evidence. Explain customer impact, privacy, ambiguity, disagreement, mechanisms, and L5 ownership.

### Hands-on outcome

Complete a five-session mock loop, present the capstone, deliver an executive summary and technical briefing, prepare eight behavioral stories, and revise the gap tracker.

### Interview outcome

Handle deep follow-up, change direction when evidence contradicts an assumption, and connect red-team work to measurable defensive improvement and cross-team ownership.

### Required artifact

Full mock-loop package: five scorecards, capstone presentation, eight story worksheets, self-critique, and remediation plan for weak dimensions.

### Completion test

No session has a critical safety or integrity miss; aggregate performance meets the locally defined threshold; and every technical claim can be traced to an artifact, primary source, or explicit inference.

### Estimated time

10 additional focused hours including review.

## Level 4: Deep, 6-week checkpoint

### Knowledge outcome

Teach complex material, handle skeptical cross-functional follow-up, present original research, distinguish local evidence from production recommendations, and propose a credible first-90-day research plan.

### Hands-on outcome

Teach one module, present the portfolio research, run two additional full mock loops, compare score trends, and write a first-90-day proposal tied to safe discovery, one bounded task, and a repeatable mechanism.

### Interview outcome

Demonstrate independent ramp-up, calibrated confidence, technical depth, customer judgment, cross-team influence, and a realistic plan for learning unknown systems before changing them.

### Required artifact

Recorded teaching session, portfolio presentation, two mock-loop packages, score trend analysis, and first-90-day research proposal.

### Completion test

The teaching is technically accurate and comprehensible, follow-up exposes no hidden overclaim, mock trends improve or explain plateaus, and the 90-day plan has measurable but environment-agnostic outcomes.

### Estimated time

8–12 additional focused hours.

## Common misconceptions

- Memorized acronyms are not depth.
- “We” can obscure personal ownership; name your actions without erasing the team.
- Confidence should track evidence, not the importance of the interview.
- A first-90-day plan should begin with learning systems and safety mechanisms, not announcing a redesign.

## Production limitations

Mocks cannot predict hiring outcomes or reproduce a specific interview loop. Public preparation material may change, and actual role scope, systems, stakeholders, and evaluation remain unknown until confirmed through authorized channels.

## Interview questions

1. Why this role, and how does your prior work reduce the ramp risk?
2. Explain one finding from hypothesis through measurable retest.
3. What would you do in your first 90 days without assuming access or authority?

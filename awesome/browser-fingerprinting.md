# Browser fingerprinting and signals

- [MDN Web APIs](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API) — `[L1 Foundation]` `[Required]` · Module 4 · Official reference. Documents browser-exposed interfaces such as Navigator, screen, canvas, WebGL, permissions, and timing. It matters for understanding what a local sensor actually observes.
- [FPScanner](https://github.com/antoinevastel/fpscanner) — `[L2 Applied]` `[Required]` · Module 4 · Research project. Explores browser inconsistencies and altered properties. It matters as a concrete example of consistency checks and their limitations.
- [CreepJS](https://github.com/abrahamjuliot/creepjs) — `[L2 Applied]` `[Required]` · Module 4 · Research project. Collects many browser characteristics and lies/noise indicators. It matters for studying signal families; use only the official repository and local authorized targets.
- [Rebrowser Bot Detector](https://github.com/rebrowser/rebrowser-bot-detector) — `[L2 Applied]` `[Recommended]` · Module 4 · Research prototype. Demonstrates automation-related checks. It matters as an inspectable example, not a universal detection benchmark.
- [OpenWPM](https://github.com/openwpm/OpenWPM) — `[L3 Integrated]` `[Optional]` · Modules 3–4 · Research project. A browser measurement platform with instrumentation and data collection. It matters for comparing research architecture and reproducibility.
